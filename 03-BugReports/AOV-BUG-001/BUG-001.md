## Description

An excessively large area of permanent vision appears to be granted to both teams throughout the match.

This vision exists even when there are no allied heroes, minions, towers, summoned units, or vision-providing skills present in the affected area.

As a result, players are able to observe enemy preparations that should normally remain hidden under the Fog of War system.

---

## Preconditions

- Enter any standard 5v5 three-lane map.
- Match has started.

No additional actions are required.

---

## Reproduction Steps

1. Start any standard 5v5 match.
2. Observe areas that should normally remain hidden under Fog of War.
3. Compare visible information with the expected vision range provided by heroes, towers, or minions.

---

## Expected Result

Players should only receive vision from legitimate in-game vision sources, including:

- Allied Heroes
- Allied Minions
- Allied Towers
- Vision Skills
- Other intended vision mechanics

Areas outside those sources should remain covered by Fog of War.

---

## Actual Result

A significantly larger area is continuously visible despite the absence of any legitimate vision source.

Enemy movement and strategic preparations can be observed in locations that should remain hidden.

---

## Impact

This issue directly affects competitive gameplay by exposing information that should remain hidden.

Potential impacts include:

- Early detection of ambushes
- Easier objective control
- Reduced effectiveness of split push strategies
- Loss of strategic uncertainty
- Reduced value of vision control mechanics

Because Fog of War is one of the core mechanics of MOBA games, incorrect vision significantly alters intended gameplay and competitive balance.

## Tester Notes

### Observation

The issue appears to exist immediately after entering a match and remains throughout gameplay.

No specific hero, item, or skill is required to trigger the behavior.

### Possible Root Cause (Hypothesis)

A default vision source may be unintentionally providing a larger reveal radius than intended.

The vision could originate from a global map object or an incorrectly configured default vision provider.

---

## Recommendation

The Fog of War system should be verified against the intended vision sources to ensure no unintended default reveal area exists on standard 5v5 maps.

---

## Tester Observation

During testing, the revealed area appears significantly larger than the expected vision range.

Although the exact source of the vision cannot be confirmed, no visible gameplay element (heroes, towers, minions, or skills) appears to justify the revealed information.
